Barter System - Where it all started . . .

~> In ancient times when the concept of money wasn't evolved, people used to transact through the Barter System of exchange. It implies the direct exchange of goods without the use of money. It's also known as the C-C Economy i.e., Commodity for Commodity Economy.

Drawbacks of the Barter System?

~> The double coincidence of wants was the major drawback of the Barter System of Exchange. It was be very difficult to find a person who was able to fulfill this condition.
~> There was no common value of exchange of goods in this system.

Gold - The next step . . .

~> To solve the double coincidence of wants, people came up with the idea to use gold as a common form of currency.It meant that anyone could buy anything without searching the right person to buy from. Gold was often used in the form of coins and bars.

Drawbacks of Gold as a currency?

~> Due to it's limited availability and high cost, its usage was reduced and new forms of currencies were intoruced.

Ancient Coins - First form of centralised currency . . .

~> Due to the limited availability and high cost of gold, coins made out of iron, nickel, etc. were introduced. Though golden coins were still prevelent but were considered as a higher form of currency having a higher value than the other coins. Kings often were found to make their citizen use custom coins inscribed with some marking of them in their territory.

Drawbacks of Ancient Coins?

~> Since all the coins were made out of metals and there were no decent alternatives, cheaper metals also started becoming more and more precious day-by-day. They also were very heavy and hard to carry around. Fake coins also were easy to make.

Paper notes and coins - The ones you use!

~> To make the usage of currency easier, paper notes were introduced which were lighter and easier to carry around and couldn't be copied easily. Again every geographic location has different forms of notes and coins in their own languages.

Drawbacks of Paper notes and coins?

~> Since most of the money stays with the owner himself, theft is common and there's no real way of keeping a check on people's balances.

Plastic money - CARDS!

~> To solve the issue of common theft and to keep a check on people's balances, banks became more prevelant and people started to deposit most of their money in a savings account in return of which they get a debit card usually encripted with a 4-digit PIN. People can easily use this card to withdraw money through ATMs (Automated Teller Machines) and pay with a simple hand-held card machine. Credit cards are also available which are basically a form of 'buy now pay later' form of exchange.

What does an SHG mean ?

~> The term SHG stands for a Self-Help-Group. It is a modern form of financing the small finance needs of borrowers who can't obtain loan from banks because of their inadequate financial documents and lack of collateral. It's usually a small group consisting of mainly 15-20 people (usually women) belonging to the same neighbourhood, who meet and save regularly.

How does an SHG work ?

~> Each member saves some amount of money ranging from 25-100 rupees or more, depending on their ability to save regularly. All the members then pool their savings, and the amount so collected is utilised to lend it to any member who needs it.

What happens if an SHG can't provide loans ?

~> If an SHG isn't able to fulfil one of its member's demand, it can get a loan from a bank with lesser or no collateral with the help of special schemes provided by the banks approved by the RBI.

What are cooperative societies ?

~> A cooperative is "an autonomous association of persons united voluntarily to meet their common economic, social, and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly-owned enterprise".

How are Cooperative societies and SHGs different ?

~> Both SHG and Cooperative societies, save money and can get loans from bank. The difference that lies here is that SHG's are usually of poor people, and found in rural areas, whereas cooperative societies are found in urban areas and consist of workers, industrial cooperatives etc.
